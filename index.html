A VISUALISATION ON SKYSCRAPERS

Skyscrapers are definitely one of the most impressive structures ever made by mankind, with the Burj Kalifa soaring over 800 metres in height.
Mordern sources currently define skyscrapers as being at least 100 metres or 150 metres in height, for this project, we are defining a skyscraper as any building with height â‰¥ 150m, and the skyscrapers used are only completed ones.
In this visualisation, we will go through the number of skyscrapers in the World, the top 10 skyscrapers, and the number and height of skyscrapers per year.

Number of Skyscrapers in the World

The proportional symbol map shows the number of Skyscapers for every Country.
China is currently destroying the numbers competition, with 2562 skyscrapers, roughly 3 times the number runner up United States of America has.

Number of Skyscrapers per 10 Million Population

When this number is compared to population size, China ranks further down the list and United Arab Emirates comes up on top. Interestingly, it can be seen that Australia is doing relatively well.

Tallest Skyscrapers in the World

Showing the top 10 tallest skyscrapers in the world, Burj Kalifa is rank 1 in terms of height, and surprisingly it was one of the cheapest to construct.
Looking at the third tallest skyscraper Makkah Royal Clock Tower, it costed roughly 10 times more than Burj Khalifa.

Note: Tianjin CTF Finance Centre has no cost data available.

Number of Skyscrapers per Year

In the US, there was a boom of skyscrapers in 1927 before plateauing all the way to 1960, this trend is most likely due to World War 2. Following its strong economic growth in the 1990s, China saw an insane rise of skyscrapers.

Height of Skyscrapers per Year

China, US, and Australia are increasing the height of their skyscrapers every year, however, In the United Arab Emirates, after the construction of Burj Khalifa in 2010, there hasn't been much change in the size of their skyscrapers.
